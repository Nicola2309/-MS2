Spring Waters and Spa's

  • Bagni San Filippo

    Water depositing calcium carbonate for thousands of years crafted this beautiful creation. In the woods of Bagni San Filippo people get together to enjoy a day, or a night, in these wonderful free thermal waters

  • Terme del Mulino

    The sulfur water in Saturnia gifted us with another creation. The several pools allow thousands of people every year to relax in these waters freely

  • Terme di Saturnia

    The pools of this Resort in Saturnia cover around 3,800 square meters, the clean water gets in the pools directly from the Earth, at a pace of 500 liters per second

  • ADLER Thermae Resort

    It is a luxury Spa immersed in the rural Tuscan landscape. The Spa offers a wide variety of treatments, including Yoga sessions and excursions in the nature around it, topping up its services with a rooftop restaurant

  • Fonteverde

    This Spa in San Casciano dei Bagni offers its treatments mainly indoor, from the Etruscan Salt Cave to the Saunas and Massages. The pools extend to the outdoor becoming a Hydro-massage Balcony on the green landscape

  • Grotta Giusti

    This unique Spa located in Monsummano has a large outdoor hot pool, and a Spa entirely located inside a calcium carbonate cave, with a divable natural pool

  • Le Caldane

    These thermal waters are located in Gracciano, they form a little lake. They have not a high temperature, and they are very pleasant to enjoy in the Summer

  • Mercure Spa

    The Mercure Hotel brand is present in 68 Countries but only in Monticciano has a thermal pool. They have other 5 different pools both inside and outside

  • Terme di Petriolo

    This Thermal site in Monticciano is a bit hard to walk to but its worth it. The hot waters lay next to the cold waters of a little crystal clear river

  • Terme Tettuccio

    This palace from 1779 in Montecatini has a modest offer in Thermal treatments, but the experience is linked to the High Society of that time and the atmosphere of their architecture

In Villages and Cities

  • Radicofani

    This little village is one of the oldest strategic locations of the area. Situated in the Val d'Orcia park, its tower was first built in 973. Nowadays the tower dominating the valley is a Museum

  • Pitigliano

    Pitigliano is one of the most iconic villages in South Tuscany, and in Italy. The 'Vie Cave' are ancient Etruscan roads digged in Tufo rocks that connect the village to Sovana, a trekking must.

  • Labirinto di Porsenna

    Underground the city of Chiusi there is a highly intricate labirinth reported by ancient historians as the Mausoleum of Lars Porsenna, an Etrurscan king. It was instead an ancient system to provide clean water to the city.

  • Pienza

    This little village is declared World Heritage Site by the UNESCO. Its center was entirely rebuilt by the Renaissance architecht Bernardo Rossellini appointed by Pope Pius II to build an "Ideal Renaissance City".

  • San Gimignano

    Another UNESCO World Heritage Site. San Gimignano holds today 14 towers built by powerful noble families around the 13nth century, the architecture of the time is preserved as it was with a few Romanesque and Gothic integrations

  • Monteriggioni

    A Medieval walled town which, despite its reduced dimensions, faced several sieges but was never conquered in its all history. Its walls and towers are part of the city museum today

  • Piazza del Campo

    Siena's center is also a World Heritage Site. The city has been one of the wealthiest and powerful cities throughout Tuscany's history, its Dome and its square are just two of the beautiful things to visit in Siena

  • Piazza del Duomo

    Piazza del Duomo in Pisa is world famous and it doesn't need any additional presentation. Pisa has its own airport and this city might be the perfect start for a vacation in Tuscany.

  • Torre Guinigi

    This tower is the last of the 250 towers built in the 13nth century, it is inside the walled center of the city. Lucca has been an Independent State till 1799, when fell in Napoleon's hands. This city has a lot to offer.

  • Santa Maria del Fiore

    The main Cathedral of Florence is one of the city's iconic symbols. As Pisa, Florence doesn't need any additional presentation. The city has also an airport to land in and begin the relaxing trip in Tuscany

Recommended Restaurants

  • Hostaria del Ceccottino

    In the center of Pitigliano, near Saturnia and Sorano, this Osteria uses only products of its area, the pasta served is homemade and it has a vast variety of wines. They have also 5 rooms to rest in and stay for the night

  • Ristorante Daniela

    In San Casciano this restaurant is located in the rooms of a castle, their dishes are also made with zero kilometers ingredients including their own products such as wine from the vineyard outside the restaurant and EVO oil

  • Osteria lo Spugnone

    This little restaurant in Bagni di San Filippo is the most classical osteria, the owners are genuine people. They have a minimal traditional menu and some wines.

  • Le Due Cecche

    This restaurant in the little village of Pari is run by two ladies, Sandra and Barbara, as "Le Due Cecche". The restaurant dishes are very simple but perfectly executed, a nice place to stop in after a bath in Petriolo's hot springs

  • Al Vecchio Forno

    This Trattoria is in a 16nth century building that has been the bakery of San Quirico d'Orcia for centuries. Their products are locally sourced and they offer a variety of 250 carefully chosen wine etiquettes in their cellar

  • Sette di Vino

    This restaurant in Pienza doesn't have any pasta, pizza or cappuccino. The owner serves grilled pecorino cheese and meat, and other traditional dishes locally sourced. A must in the city

  • Osteria Acquacheta

    This Osteria is highly recommended to meat lovers. Their dishes are based on Tuscan bovine meat cooked in many ways. The most popular is on the grill, the 'Fiorentina', a veal steak that can reach 1.7 kilos per portion

  • Agriturismo ischieto

    This fantastic restaurant is in a restored farmhouse, built in the 1400. The traditional dishes served have a gourmet touch shaped by Fiorenzo, the owner and chef, and his experience.

  • Osteria Antico Travaglio

    The Antico Travaglio Osteria in Monteriggioni makes its own pasta, and among the grilled meats offers a Chianina steak, a typical bovine of Siena, and a brilliant Chianti stew

  • Casa di Gala

    Casa di Gala in Montecatini is known for its perfectly executed dishes combined with high quality wines and its interior design which changes according to every season of the year

  • Le Vecchie Mura

    Le Vecchie Mura in San Gimignano has one of the most beautiful views of the city center. Among their specialities they serve 'Ribollita', a typical Tuscan soup, and Wild Boar stewed with Vernaccia wine produced around the city
